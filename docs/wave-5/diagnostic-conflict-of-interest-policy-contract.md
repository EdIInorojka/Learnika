# Diagnostic conflict-of-interest policy placeholder contract

## Purpose

This contract defines the static placeholder boundary for a future diagnostic
conflict-of-interest policy for Russian mathematics in grades 7-9. It makes
unresolved relationship taxonomy, disclosure, recusal, escalation, audit and
enforcement-timing requirements machine-validatable without approving a
policy, identifying a person, evaluating a relationship or activating runtime
authorization.

The contract creates no candidate, identity, reviewer assignment, conflict,
disclosure, recusal, waiver, exception, audit event, review decision or
production approval. Passing its validator is not evidence that a conflict
evaluation occurred or that a person is conflict-free.

## Contract status

The policy remains `UNRESOLVED_DEFERRED`. The activation prerequisite
`conflict_of_interest_policy` remains `UNSATISFIED_DEFERRED`, retains
`UNASSIGNED_OWNER_PLACEHOLDER` and has no evidence references.

Creating and validating this placeholder must not:

- satisfy or advance any activation prerequisite;
- classify a real relationship or approve a disqualifying criterion;
- collect, store or resolve a disclosure or personal identity;
- assign, recuse, reassign or authorize a reviewer;
- grant an exception, waiver, appeal or escalation authority;
- authorize a review decision or production approval;
- activate candidate intake, review workflow or readiness integration.

## Reviewer self-disclosure placeholder

A future approved policy must define who must disclose, when disclosure is
required, the private reference format, authorized access, completeness and
freshness requirements, correction, renewal, retention and deletion.

Slice 7 selects no disclosure format or timing rule and permits no disclosure
submission, storage, resolution, assignment evaluation or decision evaluation.
Names, email addresses, account identifiers and free-form personal details
must not enter ordinary curriculum artifacts.

## Candidate-author relationship placeholder

A future policy must define how candidate authorship and reviewer or approver
identity are bound, which relationships are relevant to one governed candidate
and version, and how unresolved identity or authorship fails closed.

`CANDIDATE_AUTHOR_ACTOR_PLACEHOLDER` and
`REVIEWER_ACTOR_PLACEHOLDER` are abstract non-authorizing actor classes. They
are not real roles, people, candidates, identities or assignments. Slice 7
records no authorship or relationship and performs no identity comparison.

## Protected record boundary

disclosures, recusals, reassignments, waivers, exceptions, escalation cases,
appeals, audit events, review decisions, approved decisions and production
approvals all remain absent. Every corresponding array is empty and every
aggregate count is zero.

Unknown, missing, populated, active or authorizing fields fail closed. Email,
UUID, user/account-ID, candidate-ID and hash-like values are rejected, as are
answer, scoring, learner-data, provider-payload and copied-content material.

## Deterministic validation expectations

The Slice 7 validator must:

- validate the full Slice 2-6 and Wave 4 authority/workflow chain;
- require exact upstream artifact and policy versions;
- require the exact unsatisfied `conflict_of_interest_policy` prerequisite;
- require the exact seven-role authority taxonomy without adding a role;
- require exactly seven unique placeholder conflict categories;
- require exactly ten unique unresolved decision requirements;
- reject active disclosure, relationship, recusal or evaluation rules;
- keep escalation authority unassigned and disabled;
- prevent any waiver or exception from authorizing production or a missing
  gate;
- require every protected record array empty and every count zero;
- keep activation, workflow and readiness at their blocked baseline;
- reject forbidden fields, private identifiers and hash-like values;
- enforce an exact static worktree allowlist and reject API, OpenAPI, Prisma,
  web, runtime, workspace-manifest and lockfile paths.

Passing validation proves only internal consistency of the placeholder. It does
not approve policy, satisfy a prerequisite, prove conflict-free status or make
review activation ready.
