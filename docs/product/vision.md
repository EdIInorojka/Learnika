# Product vision

 

## Mission

Make personalized learning support accessible to every child while preserving independent thinking and reducing repetitive work for parents and teachers.

 

## Long-term vision

Learnika accompanies a learner from age 3 to 18 through age-appropriate products on a shared platform. It understands curriculum, selected textbooks, school pace, knowledge gaps, solution behavior and goals.

 

The system connects:

 

- child;

- parent or legal representative;

- mentor and teacher;

- textbook and curriculum;

- school and class;

- assessments and examinations.

 

## Product principles

 

### 1. Independence before convenience

The system helps the learner perform the next meaningful step. It does not optimize for copying an answer.

 

### 2. Evidence before labels

A learner is not simply “weak” or “strong.” The platform records evidence by skill, recency, independence and transfer.

 

### 3. One knowledge profile, many learning paths

Textbooks, classes and exams are routes over a canonical skill graph. Changing a textbook must not erase learning history.

 

### 4. Human support at the right moment

Automation handles repetition and immediate feedback. Mentors and teachers handle ambiguity, motivation, complex explanation and high-stakes decisions.

 

### 5. Parent clarity

Reports must be short, concrete and actionable.

 

### 6. Teacher leverage

School tools reduce preparation and checking work while returning results into personalized remediation.

 

### 7. Age-appropriate design

Preschool, primary, middle and upper school require different UX, duration, autonomy and motivation. A visual skin is not a product adaptation.

 

### 8. Safety by design

Child data minimization, explicit consent, tenant isolation and safe AI behavior are platform requirements, not later additions.

 

### 9. Original and lawful content

The product builds its own content and stores textbook mappings as metadata unless rights explicitly permit more.

 

### 10. Multimodal without shortcuts

Learners may type, photograph or speak, but all input methods follow the same attempt, hint and transfer rules. Convenience must not become answer delivery.

 

### 11. Measured educational value

Success is improvement in independent performance, not minutes watched, audio recorded or tasks clicked.

 

## North-star outcome

 

A learner can solve a new problem independently after receiving platform support on a related problem.

 

## North-star metric

 

The primary educational north-star metric is:

 

> percentage of supported sessions followed by a correctly solved transfer problem without a higher hint level.

 

This metric is segmented by grade, skill, initial mastery, problem type and number of hints.
