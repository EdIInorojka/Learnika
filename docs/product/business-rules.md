# Business and product rules

 

## 1. Learning integrity

BR-001. Student mode never displays the final answer or full solution of the original homework problem.

 

BR-002. A learner must make a meaningful attempt before receiving the next hint level.

 

BR-003. Assisted completion and independent transfer are stored as separate evidence.

 

BR-004. Mastery does not change from one correct or incorrect answer.

 

BR-005. A high number of hints lowers the independence signal but does not punish the learner.

 

BR-006. Unsupported or uncertain content is declined or escalated rather than guessed.

 

BR-007. Suspected active test or examination content uses a restricted assistance policy.

 

## 2. Curriculum and textbooks

BR-101. Canonical skills are independent of textbook editions.

 

BR-102. A textbook mapping is versioned and reviewed.

 

BR-103. The platform stores textbook metadata, section references and mappings in the normal case.

 

BR-104. Full textbook text, images or copied exercises require recorded rights.

 

BR-105. Changing a textbook does not erase mastery history.

 

BR-106. School pace may prioritize plan items but cannot remove essential prerequisites.

 

## 3. Family and child accounts

BR-201. An adult controls legal consent, payment and child-account recovery.

 

BR-202. Child profiles collect only information needed for learning and safety.

 

BR-203. A parent sees learning summaries and safety signals, not unnecessary private content.

 

BR-204. A child cannot access payment, consent or adult administration functions.

 

BR-205. Deletion, export and retention rules apply by data category and legal basis.

 

## 4. Voice input

BR-251. Voice input is optional and always has a typed-input fallback.

 

BR-252. Recording begins only after explicit user action; continuous and background listening are prohibited in the MVP.

 

BR-253. The transcript is editable and requires explicit learner confirmation.

 

BR-254. Only confirmed text enters the educational assistant.

 

BR-255. Mathematical speech normalization is a proposal, not authoritative input, until confirmed.

 

BR-256. Raw audio is temporary by default and is deleted on the configured retention schedule.

 

BR-257. Raw audio and full transcripts are excluded from ordinary analytics and logs.

 

BR-258. Voice biometrics, emotion recognition and advertising profiling are prohibited.

 

## 5. Gamification

BR-301. Rewards are internal to the platform.

 

BR-302. Rewards are earned for mastery, improvement, self-correction, transfer and plan completion.

 

BR-303. Time spent, repeated clicking, recording duration or passive viewing do not generate meaningful rewards.

 

BR-304. Rankings are optional, age-appropriate and privacy-preserving.

 

BR-305. Randomized monetized loot mechanics are prohibited.

 

## 6. Human support

BR-401. Automation handles immediate repetitive feedback; specialists handle ambiguity, motivation and complex explanations.

 

BR-402. A system recommendation for a paid lesson must state the educational reason and must not be triggered solely by revenue goals.

 

BR-403. Mentor and teacher access is limited to assigned learners or classes.

 

BR-404. Sensitive access and export are audited.

 

## 7. Subscription and entitlements

BR-501. Entitlements determine subject, upload, AI, voice, mentor and assessment limits.

 

BR-502. Usage limits are visible before the learner reaches them.

 

BR-503. Cancellation is simple and does not hide already earned learning history.

 

BR-504. Payment status does not delete educational records; access changes according to retention policy.

 

BR-505. Free diagnostic results show useful information without intentionally creating fear.

 

## 8. School tenancy — future gate

BR-601. A school organization cannot access family data outside a documented relationship and consent model.

 

BR-602. School and family contexts are explicitly separated in authorization.

 

BR-603. Teacher-created grades require teacher confirmation for ambiguous or open responses.

 

BR-604. Printed assessment recognition creates confidence and manual-review states.

 

BR-605. Results may update learning recommendations only under the configured school-family data policy.
