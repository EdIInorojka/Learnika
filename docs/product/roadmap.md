# Product roadmap

 

The roadmap is gate-based, not date-only. A later stage starts only after the previous stage meets educational, product, economic, legal and operational criteria.

 

## Stage 0 — Discovery, prototype and architecture

### Output

- validated current scope and non-goals;

- clickable web prototype;

- homework and voice-input usability tests;

- architecture and domain model;

- threat model and PII inventory;

- skill-graph seed specification;

- contracts for the first vertical slice;

- test and AI evaluation strategy;

- prioritized backlog.

 

### Gate

No unresolved critical conflict between product, method, security and architecture. Learners understand the no-answer and transcript-confirmation model.

 

## Stage 1 — Technical foundation

### Output

- mobile-first web application shell;

- family and child accounts;

- consent and role model;

- textbook seed catalog;

- API and Python service foundations;

- PostgreSQL, Redis, queues and private object storage;

- CI, migrations, audit and observability baseline;

- one-command local environment.

 

### Gate

Onboarding E2E passes; tenant isolation and security baseline are approved.

 

## Stage 2 — Homework helper and web voice input

### Output

- upload and sanitization;

- task recognition and normalized schema;

- first supported algebra types;

- step validation;

- hint policy and answer guard;

- transfer task;

- short voice recording;

- asynchronous transcription;

- mathematical speech normalization;

- editable transcript confirmation;

- temporary audio deletion;

- skill evidence event.

 

### Gate

Math accuracy, answer leakage, transcription correction rate, deletion compliance, cost and user comprehension meet beta thresholds.

 

## Stage 3 — Diagnostic and personal plan

### Output

- seed skill graph;

- original diagnostic;

- mastery model;

- school, restorative and target tracks;

- weekly planner and review;

- parent report;

- analytics experiments;

- first textbook mapping workflow.

 

### Gate

Learners show repeat use and transfer improvement; parents understand reports.

 

## Stage 4 — Native mobile application

### Output

- React Native and Expo application;

- native camera and microphone workflows;

- secure token storage;

- resilient uploads and local retry;

- push notifications;

- mobile analytics and crash reporting;

- parity for approved learner and parent journeys.

 

### Gate

Mobile reliability, accessibility, store readiness and retention improvement justify continued investment.

 

## Stage 5 — Paid beta

### Output

- plans, subscriptions and entitlements;

- mentor escalation;

- support tools;

- content review workflow;

- backup, incident and cost controls;

- PII-safe product analytics and unit-economics reporting.

 

### Gate

Purchase conversion, 30/60-day retention and gross-margin path are within approved ranges.

 

## Stage 6 — Complete mathematics 7-9 and OGE

### Output

- broader algebra and geometry coverage;

- priority textbook mappings;

- OGE framework and practice blueprints;

- proof and open-response rubrics;

- performance and cost optimization;

- mature content operations.

 

### Gate

Product-market-fit evidence in the target segment and sustainable content operations.

 

## Stage 7 — School beta

### Output

- organizations, classes and teacher assignments;

- assignment and assessment builder;

- online delivery and class analytics;

- PDF variants and answer keys;

- OMR prototype and manual-review queue;

- import, export, school audit and licensing.

 

### Gate

Design-partner schools renew, teacher time is reduced and tenant isolation is verified.

 

## Stage 8 — Mathematics 5-11, EGE and age expansion

Expand down to grades 5-6 and up to grades 10-11. Add EGE basic and profile mathematics, admission goals and advanced written checking.

 

### Gate

Separate curriculum packages, UX validation by age and profitable acquisition or school distribution.

 

## Stage 9 — Russian language and primary school

- Russian language grades 7-11 first;

- mathematics, Russian and reading grades 1-6;

- parent-supported primary UX;

- text-to-speech where methodologically justified;

- VPR and school readiness for primary grades.

 

## Stage 10 — STEM, languages and advanced voice

- informatics;

- physics;

- chemistry;

- biology;

- English and additional languages;

- pronunciation, listening and controlled voice dialogue;

- interactive simulations and virtual laboratories.

 

Each subject requires a method owner, content pipeline and subject-specific checker strategy.

 

## Stage 11 — Preschool ages 3-6

Separate parent-led, tablet-first and voice-first product for early literacy, speech, counting, attention, memory and school readiness. It must not reuse middle-school UX as a visual reskin.

 

## Stage 12 — Full educational ecosystem and high-load platform

- all main school subjects;

- advanced and olympiad products;

- career guidance;

- regional and publisher partnerships;

- mature B2B and B2G integrations;

- API and white-label where strategically justified;

- service extraction, event streaming and analytical infrastructure only when scale criteria are met.

 

## Universal expansion gate

A new age, subject or voice capability requires:

 

- quantified demand;

- curriculum package;

- method owner;

- original content;

- gold evaluation set;

- learning-effect evidence;

- support and operations plan;

- unit-economics model;

- security and legal review.
