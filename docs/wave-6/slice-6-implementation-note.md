# Wave 6 / Slice 6 implementation note

## Scope

Slice 6 adds only a static, non-production audit identity policy decision
proposal, one versioned JSON artifact, a dependency-free validator, focused
tests, exact root-test registration and cumulative exact scope-guard updates.

No API, OpenAPI, Prisma, migration, database, web, runtime, dependency or
lockfile change is included.

## Preserved baseline

- proposal status: `PROPOSED_DEFERRED`;
- prerequisite `audit_identity_policy`: `UNSATISFIED_DEFERRED`;
- readiness: `NOT_READY`;
- activation: `BLOCKED`;
- workflow: `INACTIVE`;
- blockers: `INCOMPLETE_COVERAGE`, `NON_PRODUCTION_FIXTURES`;
- satisfied prerequisites: `0`;
- approved candidates: `0`;
- production approvals: `0`.

No principal, account, reviewer, audit identity, reference value, binding,
assignment, disclosure, authorization snapshot, audit event, attribution,
access grant, export, correction, amendment, evidence, review decision,
approval or governed content is created.

## Validation design

The validator exact-pins the Wave 5 activation, audit-identity and evidence
storage/retention placeholders, the Wave 6 Slice 3 role proposal, the Wave 6
Slice 4 separation proposal, the Wave 6 Slice 5 conflict proposal, and the
Wave 4 authority and workflow placeholders. It enforces twelve unresolved
decisions, non-authorizing Slice 4 and Slice 5 dependencies, exact symbolic
markers, rejected operational vectors, empty operational records, zero
operational counts, private/runtime/content rejection and an exact 44-path
Slice 6 worktree boundary.

Passing validation does not approve a policy, satisfy a prerequisite, activate
review, change readiness, close Wave 6 or start Slice 7.
